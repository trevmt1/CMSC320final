Our Question:¶

Not everyone is an NBA or College basketball fan, but a data scientist should be fan of the massive amounts of data availbe in both sectors that can be analyzied. This large amount of data gives us data scientists to test many of the common statements that those around the game of basketball make. As college and NBA basketball fans ourselves we often hear commentators and reporters making claims about how young players will develop with nothing to back it up. One of these claims that has long been floating around the basketball world is "he shoots well on his free throws in college so he will probably develop into a good NBA three point shooter". If you watch a game of college basketball or the NBA draft you'll often hear this said about prospects and young players in the NBA. However, as data scientists we were always left wondering, is this claim really true? A free throw is a vastly different shot from a three pointer. The free throw is taken closer to the basket and without defenders actively guarding the player. The three pointer is taken in the middle of a play with many different variables impacting the shot like defenders, quality of the pass, if the player is moving or set, and many others. So we set out to test this claim by compiling both NBA and college basketball stats for every player who has been drafted in the last 10 years.

The reason the question we have proposed is of useful significance is twofold. One: it pertains to the casual audience. With such a big market and fanbase that surrounds March Madness and the NBA, there is a lot of room for fans to grow attachments and follow the careers of select players from college to the professional league. This question allows fans to predict whether or not their favorite players will perform well past college. Basketball players growing up, whether recreational or professionally-motivated, can learn the measures of a good basketball player in the NBA and which college statistics are real indicators. This can give an aspiring player faith that even if a certain skill isn’t their strongest right now, success in a different area might foretell success in the area they're struggling with down the line.

For our dataset we use linear regression to determine whether or not shooting percentage success in college basketball indicates success shooting three pointers over a career in the NBA. Later in this tutorial we will perform three different linear regressions to find whether college field goal percentage, college three point percentage, or college free throw percentage is the most impactful variable on NBA three point percentage.

What is Linear Regression?¶

Data science uses scientific and technological processes in order to extract knowledge and insight from data and apply it elsewhere. Linear regression could be argued to be the epitome of data science, and it is one of the most common algorithms that data scientists use which is why we have chosen it as our approach in analyzing our dataset.

Linear regression is a machine learning model that relies on an independent and dependent variable in order to predict a line of best fit and determine the most optimal values of intercept and coeffecients. The goal is to minimize error as much as possible. A multiple linear regression involves multiple independent variables for the model to find a relationship with. In our data set, we have multiple independent variables (3-point percentage, field goal percentage, college vs the NBA, etc.) which are used to analyze our dependent variable: three-point percentage in the NBA.

The benefits and importance of linear regression is that it is a prediction model, meaning that it can help businesses and individuals observe past or current patterns or predict future trends in order to benefit themselves or improve their current practices or strategies. Linear regression notes which independent variables have the most effect on the dependent variable, and helps determine which factors should be emphasized or overlooked.

In this tutorial we will be looking at regression coefficients between the big three statistics of both college and NBA basketball. A more formal definition of regression coefficients states that a positive coefficient indicates a positive relationship between the independent and dependent variable meaning that an increasing independent variable indicates an increasing dependent variable. Conversely, a negative coefficient indicates a negative correlation wherein an increasing independent variable indicates a decreasing dependent variable.

Combining our two Datasets¶

The next order of business is combining our two data sets into one data set containing all of the information that we need. The first thing we do is an inner merge of our data sets of players' names. However, just merging isn't enough as our college data is separated by year. This means for each year a player played in college they have their own row for that year. To fix this we iterate through our array and find all rows with matching player names. This allows us to create a new data frame for that player that contains either the average or the sum of their stats across all college years. Additionally we need to create our own 'college_FG%' using the formula: (data_college['twoPM'] + data_college['TPM']) / (data_college['twoPA'] + data_college['TPA']). This formula adds up total college makes and then divides it by total college attempts. After we do this we have one more step until we are finally done setting up our data and ready to work with it.

Linear Regression on College Free Throw Percentage and NBA Three Point Percentage¶

The method for performing a linear regression in python is rather simple. For most of it we can use imported libraries to automate the process for us. In the below examples of linear regression you can see we first pick two variables to perform our regression on. Then we use a function called train_test_split to break up our data into testing groups and training groups. After that we create a linear regression object and then fit this object to our two variables we are using. After that we can use .coef to find the coefficient of the regression line and print it out.

The next step is visualizing this data into a graph. To do this quickly and easily we used the Seaborn library and imported it as sb. The .regplot function in the library allows us to simply add the two variables we want to use to make our graph and our graph will be outputted. Then, all that's left is the task of adding a title and we're ready to go. This technique for computing the coefficient of linear regression and then creating a visualization of the regression is used repeatedly throughout the tutorial.

The below linear regression between college free throw percentage and NBA three-point percentage outputs a coefficient of 0.35917025 which denotes a positive correlation between the two variables. The dataset modeled a line of best fit that indicates that the higher the free throw percentage is for a player in college, the higher the three-point percentage is for them in the NBA. This may seem like we have already answered our question, free throw shooting in college does correlate to NBA three point success. However, we should still consider other variables to see if free throw percentage is a better indicator than others. If it is not better than our other variables then we are looking in the wrong place to find the best indicator for NBA three point shooting success.

Linear Regression on College Field Goal Percentage and NBA Three Point Percentage¶

We now check other college shooting statistics to find if college free throw shooting percentage is really the best indicator for NBA three point percentage. The below linear regression is between field goal percentages in college versus NBA three-point percentages. The model outputs a negative coefficient of -0.33893196 which denotes a negative relationship between the independent and dependent variable: the higher field goal percentage in college predicts a lower three-pointer percentage for a player in the NBA.

At face value this result may come off as surprising, shouldn't a better shooter from everywhere also shoot better from three? But when we really think about what the data is telling us the answer arrives. Players with high shooting percentages are often centers and forwards who take a majority of their shots very close to the basket. These close-to-the-basket shots are much easier to make than farther away which boosts centers and forwards field goal percentage. However, the trade off is most centers and forwards don't shoot or practice three point shots often and in turn their three point percentage suffers.

Linear Regression on College Three Point Percentage and NBA Three Point Percentage¶

The below linear regression model represents college three-point percentages versus NBA three-point percentages. The regression outputs a positive coefficient of 0.27631343 which indicates a positive correlation. The higher the college three-point percentage is, the higher the NBA three-point percentage tends to be.

After getting all three linear regression coefficients from comparing these different stats, free throws has come out as the highest. It is particularly interesting that a good free throw percentage in college is a stronger indicator for NBA three point percentage than college three point percentage. Before performing this analysis we would never have suspected that this was the case. It would seem to be that reporters and commentators have been correct in their estimations that free throw percentage is a good and perhaps even the best indicator for NBA three point accuracy.

What can we conclude?¶

After performing our linear regressions we can conclude that college free throw percentage is the best indicator for NBA three point success. This conclusion has effects from the amatuer level all the way to the pros. Clearly the mechanics and touch involved with making a free throw translate over to three point shooting. Amateur players can work on perfecting their free throws knowing that if they do this their three point shot is likely to develop along with it. NBA scouts can be less hesitant about taking players with low college three point percentages if their free throw numbers are good. Overall, knowing exactly which metrics correlate to others can be a powerful tool for those involved in basketball.
